
## The 7 Steps of Problem Solving

### 1. Identify Type of Users

**Purpose:** Determine **who the problem is for**.

**Outcome:** User Personas

**Responsible:** UX Researcher

---

### 2. Make Clear Define Problem

**Purpose:** Clearly define the **core problem from the user’s perspective**.

**Outcome:** Problem Statement

**Responsible:** UX Researcher, UX Strategist

---

### 3. Explore Why They Need

**Purpose:** Understand the **root cause or motivation** behind the user’s need.

**Outcome:** POV (User + Need + Because Insight)

**Responsible:** UX Strategist, Product Designer

---

### 4. Generate a Broad Range of Creative Solutions

**Purpose:** Explore **many possibilities**, not just the “right idea.”

**Outcome:**

* Brainstorming
* Information Architecture (IA)
* User Flow
* Card Sorting
* Mind Mapping
* Sketches

**Responsible:** IA and all team members

---

### 5. Decision Making

**Purpose:** Select **feasible and impactful ideas** from the pool.

**Outcome:**

* Dot Voting
* Filter ideas based on POV (User + Need + Insight)

**Responsible:** UX Strategist, Product Designer

---

### 6. Idea to Design Solution

**Purpose:** Turn selected ideas into **tangible designs or prototypes**.

**Outcome:**

* Sketch → Wireframe → Interactive Prototype

**Responsible:** Visual Designer, Interaction Designer, UX Writer, Design System

---

### 7. Usability Testing

**Purpose:** Validate the solution with **real users** and identify gaps.

**Outcome:**

* Test scenarios, observe users, conduct interviews, measure metrics
* Iterate based on user feedback

**Responsible:** All team members and users

---
